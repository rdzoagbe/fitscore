# P6B Copy Polish Guide

Use this direction across Joblytics visuals and landing sections.

## Copy principles

1. Replace vague benefit copy with a clear outcome.
   - Weak: "Improve your CV with AI."
   - Strong: "See what to change before you apply."

2. Make every visual label explain a decision.
   - Weak: "Score: 82%."
   - Strong: "82% match — apply after quick CV tuning."

3. Use recruiter/workflow language instead of generic AI language.
   - ATS fit
   - Missing keywords
   - Proof points
   - Role-specific rewrite
   - Interview kit
   - Follow-up action

4. Keep trust copy visible.
   - No LinkedIn login.
   - No recruiter marketplace.
   - Your career data stays inside your workspace.

## Recommended microcopy

- Primary CTA: "Check my CV against a job"
- Secondary CTA: "See how the workflow works"
- Analysis state: "Reading the job requirements and comparing them with your CV evidence..."
- Empty dashboard: "Start with one job description. Joblytics will build the rest of the workspace from there."
- Save state: "Saved to your career workspace."
- Upgrade context: "Upgrade when you are actively applying and need higher limits."

## Visual text replacements

- "Generic application" → "Role-specific application"
- "AI suggestions" → "Prioritized CV actions"
- "History" → "Application intelligence"
- "Tracker" → "Application pipeline"
- "LinkedIn Optimizer" → "LinkedIn profile polish"
- "CV Coach" → "CV improvement workspace"
